How to Use This Calculator

Model the awareness-treatment-control cascade and its massive cardiovascular consequences

🎯 Who Is This For?

This tool is designed for cardiologists, public health researchers, policy analysts, and NCD program managers who need to model the burden of hypertension — the world's leading modifiable risk factor for cardiovascular death. With 1.4 billion adults affected and only 23% controlled globally, hypertension drives more DALYs than any other risk factor. This calculator models the awareness-treatment-control cascade, complication costs (stroke, MI, heart failure), workforce impact, and future projections.

Quick Start

1

Select a Country

Go to Dashboard and pick a country. Defaults load for prevalence, awareness/treatment/control rates, per-patient costs, medication costs, and complication rates from NCD-RisC, WHO, and country-specific studies.

2

Customize the Cascade

The awareness-treatment-control cascade is the key lever. Adjust awareness, treatment, and control rates to model different policy scenarios. Only 4 countries achieve >50% control: Canada, Costa Rica, Iceland, and South Korea.

3

Explore Tabs

Each tab shows a different dimension. The Economic tab shows complication costs (stroke, MI, heart failure). The Workforce tab quantifies presenteeism and GDP impact.

Methodology & Data Sources

Transparency in modeling

Projection Formula

Hybrid Growth Model

Projected_HTN(year) = Current × (1 + CAGR)years × Scenario_Multiplier × (Pop_Future / Pop_Current)

Scenarios: Optimistic (0.75x) models WHO HEARTS program + population-level salt reduction achieving 15-20% prevalence reduction. Baseline (1.00x) continues current trends. Pessimistic (1.20x) reflects accelerating urbanization, processed food consumption, and sedentary lifestyles in LMICs.

Complication modeling: CV event rates (stroke, MI, HF) are applied per 1,000 hypertensives/year. Controlled patients have 35-40% lower event rates. Complication costs use country-specific hospital cost data.

Data Sources

Prevalence: NCD-RisC 2019 (age-standardized, 30-79). National health surveys where available.

Cascade: WHO GHO. STEPS surveys. Country-specific studies from Lancet, JAMA, BMJ.

Costs: WHO CHOICE. Systematic reviews of HTN cost-of-illness. USA: $131-219B national expenditure. Global: ~$820B (WHO 2023).

DALYs: GBD 2021. High SBP as risk factor: ~211M DALYs across all CV outcomes.

Limitations

Hypertension prevalence varies by measurement method (office vs ambulatory vs home BP). White-coat and masked hypertension affect accuracy. The cascade (awareness-treatment-control) represents population averages; sub-national and demographic variation is substantial. Complication rates are modeled as averages across controlled and uncontrolled patients; the true distribution is highly skewed toward uncontrolled individuals.
