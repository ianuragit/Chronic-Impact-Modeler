How to Use This Calculator

A complete guide to modeling CKD burden, from early stages through ESRD and dialysis

🎯 Who Is This For?

This tool is designed for nephrologists, public health researchers, policy analysts, healthcare economists, and NGO professionals who need to understand and communicate the multi-dimensional burden of chronic kidney disease at national or global scale. CKD is the 11th leading cause of death globally and rising — with 90% of cases undiagnosed. This calculator models prevalence, staging, dialysis burden, economic costs, workforce impact, and future projections using research-backed defaults for 12 countries.

Quick Start (3 Steps)

1

Select a Country

Go to the Dashboard tab and pick a country from the dropdown. This loads research-backed defaults for population, CKD prevalence, dialysis patients, per-patient costs by stage, wages, DALY rates, and projection parameters sourced from GBD 2023, ISN-GKHA, USRDS, and country-specific studies.

2

Customize Parameters (Optional)

Override any default to model a specific region or intervention. Adjust dialysis cost, undiagnosed rate, stage distribution, or ESRD proportion to simulate screening programs or policy changes. "Custom Values" preserves your manual inputs.

3

Explore the Tabs

Each tab presents a different dimension of the CKD burden. All charts and numbers are live-linked to the Dashboard inputs. The stage-based cost model captures the dramatic cost escalation from early CKD ($1,000–5,000/yr) to dialysis ($40,000–120,000/yr).

Methodology & Data Sources

Transparency in modeling assumptions and data provenance

Projection Formula

Hybrid Compound Growth + Scenario Model

Projected_Cases(year) = Current_Cases × (1 + CAGR)years × Scenario_Multiplier × (Pop_Future / Pop_Current)

Scenario Multipliers: Optimistic (0.60x) assumes CKD screening in diabetics reduces ESRD by 40% and SGLT2 inhibitors delay progression significantly. Baseline (1.00x) continues current trends. Pessimistic (1.25x) accounts for rising diabetes and hypertension prevalence in LMICs.

Stage Transition: CKD follows a Markov model: Stage 1-2 → Stage 3 at 1.26%/year; Stage 3 → Stage 4-5 at 1.21%/year; Stage 4 → Stage 5 at 94% over 5 years. These inform the stage distribution and cost escalation.

Data Sources

Prevalence: Global Burden of Disease Study 2023 (IHME). ISN-GKHA (International Society of Nephrology Global Kidney Health Atlas). National registry data (USRDS, UK Renal Registry, ANZDATA).

Economic Data: USRDS Annual Data Reports. Systematic reviews of CKD cost-of-illness studies (Lancet, JASN). WHO CHOICE cost-effectiveness thresholds. Country-specific dialysis cost surveys.

DALYs & Disability Weights: GBD 2021. Disability weights: CKD Stage IV = 0.104, dialysis = 0.571, post-transplant = 0.024.

Workforce: Meta-analyses of CKD employment outcomes. Dialysis employment rate of 26.3% from systematic reviews. Caregiver burden from UK and Australian cohort studies.

Limitations & Caveats

CKD prevalence estimates vary widely by detection method (eGFR vs albuminuria vs both). Stage distribution defaults are global averages; country-specific registries may differ. Dialysis costs represent enormous variation even within countries (peritoneal vs hemodialysis, public vs private). The 90% undiagnosed rate is a global estimate — high-income countries with screening programs may have lower rates. Transplant data reflects performed transplants, not demand (which far exceeds supply in most countries).
