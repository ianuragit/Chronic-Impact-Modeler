How to Use This Calculator

A complete guide to interpreting the data, understanding the model, and getting the most from each tab

🎯 Who Is This For?

This tool is designed for public health researchers, policy analysts, healthcare economists, and NGO professionals who need to understand and communicate the multi-dimensional burden of diabetes at national or global scale. It can be used for grant proposals, policy briefs, health budget planning, advocacy presentations, and academic research. Every number on every tab updates instantly when you change inputs, so you can model any country or hypothetical scenario in seconds.

Quick Start (3 Steps)

1

Select a Country

Go to the Dashboard tab and pick a country from the dropdown. This loads research-backed default values for population, prevalence, per-patient costs, wages, DALY rates, complication rates, and projection parameters. All 20 countries have individually sourced data from IDF, WHO, GBD, and country-specific studies.

2

Customize Parameters (Optional)

Override any default value to model a specific region, intervention scenario, or hypothetical. For example, set prevalence to a sub-national estimate, adjust complication rates to reflect a screening program, or change cost-per-patient to model a specific insurance pool. Choosing "Custom Values" in the dropdown preserves your manual inputs.

3

Explore the Tabs

Each tab presents a different dimension of the diabetes burden. All charts and numbers are live-linked to the Dashboard inputs. Switch between tabs to see how your inputs affect economic costs, workforce productivity, future projections, and cross-country rankings.

FUTURE MODELING Projections & Scenarios

Projects the diabetic population and economic burden from 2026 to 2056 using a hybrid model that combines compound prevalence growth (CAGR), population growth, and healthcare cost inflation. Three scenarios are available:

Optimistic (0.65x): Assumes strong public health intervention achieves a 35% reduction in disease growth, based on evidence from the Diabetes Prevention Program which achieved 58% risk reduction through lifestyle changes.

Baseline (1.00x): Current trends continue without significant policy changes. This is the default projection.

Pessimistic (1.30x): Worsening obesity rates, urbanization, and dietary shifts in LMICs accelerate diabetes growth by 30% above baseline.

Click any scenario pill to toggle it on/off in the chart. Use the sliders below the charts to adjust CAGR, population growth rate, and healthcare cost inflation for sensitivity analysis.

FINANCIAL Economic Burden

Deep-dives into where money flows. The pie chart splits total cost into direct medical, indirect productivity losses, and informal caregiver costs. The payer chart shows who bears the burden: out-of-pocket (patients/families), government/public insurance, and private insurance. These splits vary dramatically by country (India: 55% OOP vs. France: 8% OOP).

The Complication Cost Waterfall shows how per-patient costs escalate by complication type. An uncomplicated diabetic may cost $400/year in India, but a patient with nephropathy requiring dialysis costs 3.5x more, and an amputation event costs 8x more. This waterfall uses your Dashboard cost-per-patient as the baseline and applies research-derived multipliers.

Catastrophic expenditure measures how many families spend more than 10% of household income on diabetes care, often pushing them below the poverty line. In LMICs, this affects 25-40% of diabetic households.

SOCIETAL Workforce & Societal

Quantifies the hidden costs that standard health economics often misses. Four components of productivity loss are calculated:

Absenteeism: Diabetics take an average of 5.5 extra sick days per year beyond baseline. Cost = excess days × daily wage.

Presenteeism: Working while sick at reduced capacity. Research shows 6.6% productivity loss for diabetics, meaning every 15 workers lose the equivalent of 1 full worker's output.

Reduced participation: 10% of working-age diabetics drop out of the labor force entirely, per JAMA Health Forum 2025.

Caregiver productivity: Informal caregivers of complicated diabetics lose work hours. Set via the "Caregiver burden hrs/wk" slider on Dashboard.

The DALY doughnut splits disability burden into Years of Life Lost (premature death, ~55%) and Years Lived with Disability (for uncomplicated and complicated patients). The intergenerational panel highlights that children of diabetic parents face 40-70% lifetime T2D risk, and 35% of diabetics experience comorbid depression.

COMPARISON Country Comparison

A dual-axis chart showing diabetic population (bars, left axis) and cost-per-patient (line, right axis) for the top 12 countries by diabetic population. This reveals the stark inverse relationship: countries with the most diabetics (China, India) often have the lowest per-patient spending, while high-income countries spend 30-100x more per patient but have far fewer total cases.

Below the chart, a full sortable data table covers all 20 countries with population, prevalence, diabetic count, undiagnosed rate, cost per patient, national cost, and DALYs per 100K. Use this for quick reference when comparing countries in reports or presentations.

Key Metrics Glossary

DALY
Disability-Adjusted Life Year: 1 DALY = 1 year of healthy life lost to illness or premature death
QALY
Quality-Adjusted Life Year: 1 QALY = 1 year of life in perfect health (EQ-5D based)
CAGR
Compound Annual Growth Rate: the steady yearly growth rate needed to go from start to end value
Presenteeism
Working while sick at reduced productivity; costs 3x more than absenteeism overall
Catastrophic Expenditure
Health spending exceeding 10% of household income, often causing impoverishment
OOP (Out-of-Pocket)
Direct payments by patients/families not covered by any insurance or public scheme
PAF
Population Attributable Fraction: % of disease cases caused by a specific risk factor
KRT pmp
Kidney Replacement Therapy per million population (dialysis + transplant access metric)
💡 Tips for Power Users

Scenario comparison: Load a country, note the 2041 projection, then toggle between scenarios to see how investment in prevention (optimistic) vs. inaction (pessimistic) changes the outcome. The difference is often 30-50% in total diabetic population.

Sensitivity analysis: Pick one slider at a time (e.g., complication rate) and sweep it from min to max while watching the stat cards update. This reveals which parameters have the highest leverage on total burden.

Custom sub-national modeling: Select "Custom Values" from the dropdown, then input data for a specific state, province, or city. Use local survey prevalence, regional wage data, and local cost figures for high-accuracy sub-national estimates.

Theme toggle: Use the sun/moon toggle in the top-right corner of the header to switch between dark and light mode. Light mode is ideal for screenshots in reports and presentations. Dark mode reduces eye strain during extended analysis sessions.

Methodology & Data Sources

Transparency in modeling assumptions and data provenance

Projection Formula

Hybrid Compound Growth + Scenario Model

Projected_Cases(year) = Current_Cases × (1 + CAGR)years × Scenario_Multiplier × (Pop_Future / Pop_Current) × Age_Structure_Adj

Scenario Multipliers: Optimistic (0.65x) assumes strong intervention programs achieving 58% risk reduction (per DPP evidence). Baseline (1.00x) continues current trends. Pessimistic (1.30x) accounts for accelerating obesity and worsening diets in LMICs.

Cost Projections: Economic burden is projected using healthcare cost inflation rates (regional, not global averages) applied compound annually. Indirect costs use a human-capital approach valuing lost productivity at country-specific average wages.

DALY Calculation: DALYs = YLL (Years of Life Lost) + YLD (Years Lived with Disability). Disability weights from GBD 2021: uncomplicated diabetes = 0.049, neuropathy = 0.133, blindness = 0.338. DALYs are age-standardized to enable cross-country comparison.

Data Sources

Prevalence: IDF Diabetes Atlas 10th & 11th Edition (2021 & 2024). National survey data where available (NFHS for India, NHANES for USA, CNHS for China).

Economic Data: ADA Economic Costs of Diabetes 2023 (USA). IDF health expenditure estimates. Country-specific cost-of-illness studies from Lancet, BMJ, and JAMA publications. World Bank health expenditure statistics.

DALYs & Health Metrics: Global Burden of Disease Study 2021 (IHME). Disability weights from GBD 2021. Life expectancy reductions from multi-country cohort studies.

Workforce Data: JAMA Health Forum 2025 (participation reduction). ADA indirect cost methodology. WPAI (Work Productivity and Activity Impairment) studies. Caregiver burden from systematic reviews.

Projections: Population projections from UN DESA World Population Prospects 2024. Healthcare inflation from Willis Towers Watson Global Medical Trends Survey 2025.

Limitations & Caveats

This tool provides estimates for policy analysis and research purposes. Actual costs and prevalence vary by sub-national region, urban/rural setting, and healthcare system structure. Cost data may lag current values by 1-3 years. Projection models assume continuation of underlying trends and do not account for breakthrough medical innovations (e.g., GLP-1 agonists' population-level impact) or pandemic disruptions. Default values represent best available published estimates and should be verified against local data where possible.
